Kumar Sangakkara


Sri Lankan legendary cricketer

Here's a time-line of Kumar Sangakkara:

  • 1977 - Born in Matale, Sri Lanka
  • 1983 - Received his primary and secondary education at Trinity College, Kandy
  • 1996 - Awarded the highest honor of Trinity College, the Ryde Gold Medal
  • 2000 - At the age of 22, he made his test debut and finished it with at an good average of 66.33
  • 2002 - He scored his first double-centuary against Pakistan and secured victory for Sri Lanka
  • 2004 - He made a partnership of 438 for the 2nd wicket—4th highest in the world—against Zimbabwe in 2004. In that game, he scored 270, his first 250+ score
  • 2006 - In July 2006, Sangakkara made his second-highest Test score to-date (287) against South Africa. In a record-breaking partnership with Mahela Jayawardene, he set up the world record for the highset partnership in Test cricket—624 runs—in this match.
  • 2009 - At the age of 31 and with the experience of 80 Tests and 246 ODIs, Sangakkara succeeded as Sri Lanka's captain in all formats of the game.
  • 2011 - Led his team to finals of WCC and was named as captain and wicket keeper of the 'Team of the Tournament' for the 2011 World Cup by the ICC
  • 2012 - He was honored as one of the Wisden Cricketers of the Year
  • 2014 - He hits his highest test score to date with 319 against Bangladesh
  • 2014 - Scored 51 not out off just 33 balls making his team win their second ICC trophy since 1996
  • 2015 - On 27 June 2015, Sangakkara officially announced his retirement from all forms of national cricket
  • 2015 - Sri Lanka Rupavahini Corporation recognized Sangakkara as the "Player of the Century" citing many of Sangakkara's past memories and achievements
  • 2017 - Kumar Sangakkara made his ICC TV commentary debut during the 2017 ICC Champions Trophy
  • 2018 - Kumar Sangakkara has joined SkySports for commentary throughout the English summer of 2018
  • 2019 - Sangakkara becomes the first non-British MCC President since the club was founded in 1787
